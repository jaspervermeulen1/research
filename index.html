About

I am a final-year PhD candidate at Queensland University of Technology and the Australian Cobotics Centre. My research examines how people work with, around, and through robotic systems in real-world settings, with a particular focus on robot-assisted orthopaedic surgery.

Through ethnographic observation, interviews, and video-based analysis, I study how robotic surgery is made workable through distributed human coordination. My work shows that human-robot collaboration is rarely a simple dyad between one human and one robot. In practice, it often depends on surgeons, nurses, assistants, robotic specialists, instruments, displays, spatial constraints, and organisational routines working together.

My doctoral research develops a socio-technical account of human-robot collaboration in the wild, contributing to HRI, HCI, CSCW, human factors, and robotics research.

Selected publications

My publications examine human-robot collaboration as it unfolds in real-world practice, with a focus on robot-assisted surgery, multi-actor teamwork, and the often invisible human work required to make robotic systems function safely and effectively.

Jasper T. Vermeulen, James L. Dwyer, Alan G. Burden, Glenda Amayo Caldwell, Müge Belek Fialho Teixeira, Matthias Guertler, Ross Crawford

An ethnographic study of robot-assisted orthopaedic surgery showing how collaboration with a surgical robot is coordinated across surgeons, nurses, assistants, robotic specialists, instruments, displays, and the operating theatre itself.

Jasper T. Vermeulen, Glenda Caldwell, Müge Belek Fialho Teixeira, Alan Burden, James Dwyer, Matthias Guertler
