Publications

Selected and full publications

My publications examine human-robot collaboration as it unfolds in real-world practice, especially robot-assisted surgery and multi-actor teamwork.

Featured publications

ACM CHI 2026

The Choreography of Care: Human-Robot Collaboration in Makoplasty Surgery

Jasper T. Vermeulen, James L. Dwyer, Alan G. Burden, Glenda Amayo Caldwell, Müge Belek Fialho Teixeira, Matthias Guertler, Ross Crawford

An ethnographic study of robot-assisted orthopaedic surgery showing how collaboration with a surgical robot is coordinated across surgeons, nurses, assistants, robotic specialists, instruments, displays, and the operating theatre itself.

ACM DIS 2026

The Invisible Work of Robotic Surgery

Jasper T. Vermeulen, Müge Belek Fialho Teixeira, Glenda Amayo Caldwell, Alan G. Burden, Matthias Guertler

An interview-based study of Mako Product Specialists showing how robotic surgery depends on support, repair, translation, anticipation, and relational work that often remains invisible in formal accounts of human-robot collaboration.

Behaviour & Information Technology

Human-centred strategies for improving efficiency in Makoplasty surgeries

Jasper T. Vermeulen, Glenda Caldwell, Müge Belek Fialho Teixeira, Alan Burden, James Dwyer, Matthias Guertler

A video-based analysis of Makoplasty procedures identifying human-centred strategies for improving surgical efficiency, including role adaptability, team calibration, surgical gaze, and tool-assisted spatial coordination.

Jasper T. Vermeulen

A doctoral research paper positioning robot-assisted surgery as a setting for developing richer accounts of multi-actor human-robot collaboration in the wild.

RO-MAN 2026

All for One: A PRISMA Scoping Review of Team-Based Human-Robot Collaboration

Jasper T. Vermeulen, Müge Belek Fialho Teixeira, Glenda Amayo Caldwell, Alan G. Burden, Matthias Guertler

A scoping review of multi-human, one-robot collaboration across domains including manufacturing, surgery, construction, healthcare action teams, emergency response, and cross-domain laboratory research.

Forthcoming2026

Publication trajectory

Together, these publications form part of my thesis-by-publication on human-centred and multi-actor human-robot collaboration in robot-assisted surgery. Across video analysis, ethnographic observation, and expert interviews, the work develops a socio-technical account of how robotic systems are made workable through distributed human coordination.
