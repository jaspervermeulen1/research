Profile

My doctoral research examines human factors in multi-actor human-robot collaboration within surgical settings, with an emphasis on the gap between how robot-assisted surgery is designed and how it is performed in practice.

The thesis, Understanding the Human(s) in Human-Robot Collaboration: A Socio-Technical Inquiry into Robot-Assisted Surgeries, draws on video analysis of robot-assisted surgeries, 100+ hours of ethnographic observation in a Brisbane hospital, and interviews with surgical team members across Australian cities. The expected defence date is September 2026.
